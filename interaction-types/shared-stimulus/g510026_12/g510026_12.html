Few people consciously contemplate the drug that they ingest daily in their morning coffee, afternoon tea, or daily soda. However, many people consume at least some caffeine everyday; in fact, it is the world's most pervasive drug. In the United States alone, consumers spend over 50 billion dollars on caffeinated sodas alone each year. Consumers ingest caffeine in both small and large amounts: from the 25 mg in an average chocolate bar, to the 200 mg in the average cup of brewed coffee.

The widespread use of caffeine is distressing to some scientists and medical researchers. Caffeine raises blood pressure, which can in turn lead to higher rates of heart disease. Caffeine is also a diuretic, and can lead to dehydration if consumed in large amounts. Further, some studies have linked caffeine use to increased rates of cancer and osteoporosis, a disorder where calcium is leached from bones.

However, other scientists point to the many benefits of caffeine. The drug can promote alertness and reduce short term fatigue. It also has the ability to fight headache pain and improve mood. These scientists also contend that most of the purported ill effects are minimal. They point out that caffeine does not dehydrate when consumed in small amounts. Further, they suggest that caffeine's effect on blood pressure is temporary, and that the amount of calcium lost from bones could be replaced by the addition of a few tablespoons of milk to one's diet per day.

Read the Passage Strategically:

Passage Map:

Paragraph 1: Introduces caffeine as a widespread drug

Paragraph 2: Discusses the view that caffeine can be dangerous

Paragraph 3: Discusses the view that caffeine has many benefits and may not be dangerous in small amounts

Topic: Caffeine Use

Scope: The positive and negative effects of caffeine use

Purpose: To discuss some positive and negative views of caffeine

  1. The author's primary purpose is to

    1. argue that caffeine use should be outlawed in the United States
    2. show that caffeine is not a dangerous drug
    3. illustrate caffeine's many benefits
    4. discuss positive and negative views of caffeine use
    5. suggest that caffeine is safe in small doses

    Analyze the Question Stem:

    The words “primary purpose” indicate that this is a Global question.

    Research the Relevant Text:

    For Global questions, there's no need to research a specific part of the passage. Rather, consult the passage map to get a handle on the answer.

    Make a Prediction:

    Remember that the answer to a "primary purpose" question will be consistent with the author's point of view. The author here simply discusses the views of scientists who come out in favor of or against caffeine use, without asserting any opinion as to which side is right.

    Evaluate the Answer Choices:

    (D) is a good match for the prediction, and is the correct answer.

    Since there is no attempt by the author to 'argue', 'show', or 'suggest' anything, (A), (B) and (E) are out.

    (C) is incorrect because even though the author does discuss the benefits of caffeine, it's only in one paragraph. This is a detail, not the author's “primary purpose.”

    TAKEAWAY: Nearly every GMAT passage is accompanied by a “primary purpose” Global question. This is why part of the Kaplan method is to think about the author's Purpose as you read.

  2. The author mentions all of the following as potential benefits of caffeine use EXCEPT

    1. increased alertness
    2. cancer-fighting properties
    3. reduced fatigue
    4. improved mood
    5. reduced headache pain

    Analyze the Question Stem:

    The phrase “author mentions” in the stem signals a Detail question. This is an EXCEPT question, so the four wrong choices will be potential benefits of caffeine that the author explicitly states.

    Research the Relevant Text:

    The passage map indicates that the third paragraph is where the benefits of caffeine are discussed.

    Make a Prediction:

    Potential benefits of caffeine listed in paragraph 3 are: promotes alertness, reduces fatigue, fights headache pain and improves mood. Since this is an EXCEPT question, the right answer will be the choice that does NOT mention one of these.

    Evaluate the Answer Choices:

    Only (B), cancer-fighting properties, is not one of the four listed benefits of caffeine from paragraph 3. It is the correct answer.

    TAKEAWAY: In a Detail EXCEPT question, make sure you pick the answer that is not mentioned in the passage.

  3. With which of the following statements would the scientists mentioned in the third paragraph most likely agree?

    1. People who drink an extra glass of milk each day and consume a normal amount of caffeine will not suffer from increased rates of osteoporosis.
    2. Everyone who uses caffeine in moderate amounts is safe from heart disease.
    3. Caffeine has no major ill effects.
    4. A person who consumes a small amount of caffeine in the morning will immediately feel more alert and less tired than someone who foregoes caffeine.
    5. Caffeine is a powerful cancer-fighting drug.

    Analyze the Question Stem:

    The words “most likely agree” signal that this is an Inference question. Inference questions ask for the answer choice that must be true given what is stated in the passage.

    Research the Relevant Text:

    The question asks for what the scientists in the third paragraph would agree with, so look at the passage map to determine their point of view. They think that caffeine has benefits, and that the supposed drawbacks cited by other scientists aren't that bad. Since this is an open-ended question, further research may be needed based on the choices.

    Make a Prediction:

    The right answer can't be specifically predicted, but keep these scientists' point of view in mind when evaluating the choices.

    Evaluate the Answer Choices:

    (A) is the correct answer; the scientists in the third passage believe that drinking extra milk can offset the calcium loss caused by caffeine.

    (B) and (C) are both extreme statements. These scientists believe that most ill effects of caffeine are minimal, but that doesn't mean that all caffeine users are safe from heart disease or that there are no ill effects.

    (D) may be tempting, since, according to these scientists,caffeine can increase alertness and reduce fatigue. However, there's no support for the fact that this would happen "immediately" or that that a caffeine user would be more alert than anyone who hadn't had caffeine.

    The scientists mentioned in the third paragraph do not address the cancer causing or fighting properties of caffeine, so (E) is unsupported.

    TAKEAWAY: Avoid extreme statements in Inference questions, unless there is extreme support in the passage.
