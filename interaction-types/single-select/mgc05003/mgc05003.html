If a reaction rate is zero-order with respect to reactant A, how will the reaction rate change when the concentration of A is increased four-fold?

  1. Decrease by a factor of four
  2. Increase by a factor of two
  3. Increase by a factor of four
  4. The reaction rate will not change

Zero-order with respect to reactant A means that the rate of the reaction does not depend on the concentration of A. That means that if the concentration of A is increased by a factor of four, the reaction rate should not change at all.  The correct answer is (D).
